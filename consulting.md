I set up my independent consulting practice, [Outside Context Ltd](https://www.georgewalkley.com/about/), in 2020, after nearly twenty-five years in the media and publishing industry, including fifteen years in senior executive roles at the Big Five trade publisher Hachette UK. Since then I have worked with more than forty clients around the world.

Most of my consulting work sits at the intersection of two questions: *where is your industry going?* and *what should you do about it?* Those questions have always been difficult. The arrival of generative AI has made them urgent.

## What I do

My engagements typically fall into four areas:

**Strategy reviews.** I work with boards and leadership teams on publishing, content, digital and AI strategy&#8212;helping organisations assess their position, stress-test assumptions and make decisions under uncertainty. This often involves competitive analysis, scenario planning and structured facilitation.

**AI transformation.** I help organisations move from experimenting with AI tools to building deliberate, governed AI capability. This includes assessing readiness, identifying high-value use cases, designing workflows and agent architectures, and developing internal policy and governance frameworks. [I also deliver award-winning, hands-on AI training](https://www.georgewalkley.com/training/) for teams.

**Research and horizon scanning.** I produce research, analysis and briefing documents on technology trends, market shifts, regulatory developments and competitive dynamics&#8212;particularly where AI intersects with publishing, media and other knowledge-intensive industries.

**Facilitation.** I design and facilitate away days, board-level strategy sessions, and corporate events. My approach combines structured frameworks with practical exercises, and I'm experienced at working with groups that include a wide range of cultural backgrounds, seniority and technical confidence. [I'm also a highly experienced keynote speaker and event chair](https://www.georgewalkley.com/speaking/).

## Who I work with

Most of my clients are in publishing, media, education and professional services, but I increasingly work with organisations in adjacent sectors&#8212;communications, research, and membership bodies&#8212;where content, knowledge and intellectual property are central to the business. Typical clients include trade and academic publishers, industry associations, literary agencies, technology companies, and non-profits.

In 2023, I was commissioned by the [Independent Publishers Guild](https://www.independentpublishersguild.com) to develop a training course on generative AI for the publishing industry. I spent several months intensively researching the subject&#8212;talking to technology companies, investors, researchers, publishing organisations and early adopters in other sectors&#8212;and launched the training in September 2023. It was the first independent, CPD-accredited AI course designed specifically for publishing professionals. It won Best Learning/Professional Development programme at the 2024 Association Excellence Awards. Since then I have delivered it to delegates from nearly three hundred organisations in fifteen countries.

The course is updated continuously to reflect the pace of change in AI. It is not a generic introduction to ChatGPT&#8212;it is built around the specific workflows, challenges and opportunities facing publishing and media organisations.

## What the training covers

The course is structured around four areas:

**Understanding the landscape.** A clear, jargon-free overview of the AI landscape: how large language models work, what they can and can't do, and how the technology is evolving. I explain the key concepts that matter for publishing professionals without requiring any technical background.

**Issues, risks and opportunities.** The practical questions organisations need to address: copyright and licensing, editorial integrity, author relationships, data governance, competitive dynamics and regulatory developments including the EU AI Act.

**Strategy and implementation.** How to move from awareness to action: identifying high-value use cases, building internal capability, developing AI policy and governance, and making the case for investment. This section draws on real examples from organisations I've worked with.

**Hands-on practice.** Participants work directly with a range of AI tools and models, guided through proven use cases relevant to their roles. This is not a demonstration — delegates leave having built real workflows they can use immediately.
